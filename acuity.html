Visual identity
Acuity Scheduling

Squarespace acquired Acuity Scheduling in 2019. In the years since, Squarespace has integrated scheduling capabilities into its suite of selling tools, while continuing to scale Acuity as a standalone platform. My team was tasked with developing an identity for Acuity that would communicate this positioning. We were asked to equip the brand both to live on its own and to integrate smoothly into Squarespace communications.

The result pulls inspiration from across the scheduling process. The Acuity Scheduling palette responds dynamically to the passage of time: keystone gradients map to parts of the day, meeting customers where they are in real time. A composition system uses echos the language of calendar blocks. And the steady blinking colon of digital clocks acts as cornerstone for the brand, appearing in its wordmark and informing its proprietary motion. We worked closely with Acuity's team to apply this identity across surfaces, directing a photo and video shoot and developing brand expression across the product.

The Acuity palette is cyclical, mapped to the course of the day. It splits into four keystone gradients or twenty versatile spot colors.
Acuity's wordmark is set in all lowercase, confidently bisected by a colon. It evokes a digital clock or watch: modern interpreters of time.
Its typographic system pairs Clarkson — designed for the Squarespace brand ecosystem — with Recoleta, which lends its visual vocabulary a warm, conversational tone.
A layout system evoking calendar blocks adheres to a set construction process. A modular grid system dictates margins and columns. Events are placed on the grid and broken into blocks, then annotated with timestamps.
The Acuity colon evokes time in all of its forms: blinking like a clock face, orbiting like planets, or oscillating like pendulums.
Acuity photography surveys all kinds of bookable experiences. Appointments booked with Acuity are rendered without the hold music, paperwork, or waiting rooms — smooth and seamless.
In its brand communications, Acuity surfaces appear smooth and glassy, yet welcoming and warm.
